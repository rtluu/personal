VUMBLE

THE LEADERBOARD FOR ONLINE VIDEO
Role:
Founder, Product Manager & Frontend
Process:
  • 1. Problem
  • 2. Research
  • 3. Solution
  • 4. Development
  • 5. Feedback

1. Problem

It’s difficult to quickly discover videos that are worth watching on YouTube. Titles and thumbnails are often misleading (clickbait) and YouTube’s recommendations are narrow because they’re based on the things that you’ve already clicked.

2. Research

Solutions people use to solve this problem are Wimp and Reddit Videos. Wimp curates YouTube videos that are submitted by the public, but there are no community engagements (likes, comments, etc.). Viewers are left in an isolated experience with very little context as to why a video is worth viewing.

Reddit videos is a community on Reddit where users shares videos and vote to determine which ones are worth watching. The voting surfaces quality content with engaging conversation, but their viewing experience is fragmented with users leaving the site to view videos and a separate url to view comments.

How can we make it easier for people to discover videos that are worth watching?

3. Solution

A web and mobile application that pulls the best videos from across reddit and displays them in a feed that enables instant discovery.

Previous: A web and mobile application that displays 6 second previews before videos to help users decide what to watch.

4. Development

I led my team through weekly agile sprints in Waffle.io. We managed our code on Github and deployed to Digital Ocean servers.
