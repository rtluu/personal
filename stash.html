STASH

CUSTOM COLLECTIONS OF VALUABLE WEB CONTENT
Role:
Founder, Product Manager & Frontend
Process:
  • 1. Problem
  • 2. Research
  • 3. Solution
  • 4. Development
  • 5. Feedback

1. Problem

Digital creators have a difficult time collecting and revisting their valuable web content. They're forced to use different tools to save different types of content and tend to lose track of where things are saved.

2. Research

Solutions people use to solve this problem are bookmarks, Tumblr, and Pinterest.

Bookmarks aren’t visual and because they’re text heavy they quickly become cluttered. Additionally, bookmarks are stuck in your browser and can’t be shared with others.

Tumblr's search is ineffective. It doesn't query metadata making it difficult for users to revisit previously collected content.

Users can't collect all forms of content on Pinterest. It's great for images, but inefficient for collecting articles, webpages, music, etc. Pinterest also lacks customization; all pinterest boards look the same.

3. Solution

A web and mobile application that helps people create custom collections of web content that they can share with others.

Previous: A web and mobile application that helps people save content from across the web in different boards called Stashes. (View experience here).

5. Feedback

We're been gathering feedback from early adopters to better understand their collecting behavior and frustrations they face.

Some of the things people have mentioned are tags, a browser extension, and a way to see what other people are saving. We have a lot in the works including unique urls to share your Stash with others.
